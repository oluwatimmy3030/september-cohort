BOIGRAPHY OF PRESIDENT HAMMED TINUBU

EARLY LIFE

Bola Ahmed Tinubu was born on March 29, 1952, in Lagos, Nigeria. He hails from a Yoruba family and his early life was deeply rooted in the traditions and culture of the Yoruba people.His mother, Abibatu Mogaji, was a prominent market woman and the Iyaloja of Lagos, which afforded Tinubu a background in leadership and community engagement from an early age.

EDUCATIONAL BACKGROUND

Tinubu's educational journey began in Nigeria, where he attended St. John's Primary School in Aroloya, Lagos, and Children's Home School in Ibadan. He later moved to the United States for further studies. Tinubu attended Richard J. Daley College in Chicago before transferring to Chicago State University. He graduated in 1979 with a Bachelor's degree in Accounting. During his time at Chicago State University, Tinubu was recognized for his academic excellence and even received the prestigious Summa Cum Laude distinction.

POLITICAL CAREER

Tinubu returned to Nigeria in 1998, after the death of military dictator Sani Abacha and the subsequent transition to civilian rule. In 1999, he ran for and won the governorship of Lagos State under the banner of the Alliance for Democracy (AD). His tenure as governor, which lasted from 1999 to 2007, was marked by significant developments in infrastructure, education, healthcare, and public service reform in Lagos State. Tinubu is credited with laying the foundation for the modern Lagos, transforming it into a megacity and Nigeria's economic hub.

POST-GOVERNORSHIP AND ROLE IN NATIONAL POLITICS


After leaving office as governor, Tinubu remained a powerful figure in Nigerian politics. He was instrumental in the formation of the All Progressives Congress (APC) in 2013, a merger of several opposition parties aimed at challenging the dominance of the People's Democratic Party (PDP). His strategic political maneuvering played a crucial role in the APC's victory in the 2015 presidential election, which brought Muhammadu Buhari to power. Tinubu's influence in Nigerian politics has earned him the nickname "Jagaban," a title given to him by the Borgu Kingdom in Niger State. He is widely regarded as the political godfather of Lagos State and has been a kingmaker in both regional and national politics.

PRESIDENCY

In 2023, Bola Tinubu ran for the presidency of Nigeria under the APC banner. His campaign focused on economic reform, infrastructure development, and improving national security. He won the election and was inaugurated as the President of Nigeria on May 29, 2023.

PERSONAL LIFE

Tinubu is married to Oluremi Tinubu, a former first lady of Lagos State and currently a senator representing Lagos Central Senatorial District. They have several children, including Jide Tinubu, who passed away in 2017, and Seyi Tinubu, a prominent businessman.

LEGACY

Bola Ahmed Tinubu's legacy is one of resilience, strategic political acumen, and significant contributions to the development of Lagos State and Nigerian politics. Despite controversies and criticisms, particularly regarding his wealth and political influence, Tinubu remains a central figure in Nigeria's political landscape. His impact on Lagos State's transformation and his role in the emergence of a new political order in Nigeria are widely recognized.
